Working in groups of 4-5 students, acting as the newly appointed Chief AI Officer in a Railway company, with
the mission of improving operations using artificial intelligence. 

The company has provided the following input:
1. Problem Statement
 - The SRS company(railway) aims to determine whether railway switch failures can be predicted by leveraging existing IoT data, along with maintenance and fault records.
 - Accurately forecasting such failures would help reduce train disruptions, enhance the responsiveness of operations teams, improve customer satisfaction, and minimize costs associated with inefficiencies and unexpected breakdowns.

2. Key expected benefits
 1) Identify critical failure patterns and early warning signals.
 2) Achieve over **99,99%** (Irrealista) accuracy in predicting failures within 30, 90, 180, and 360 days.
 3) Generate annual **savings between €2–5 million** to justify operational changes (**savings below €2 million would not warrant implementation**).
 4) Develop an optimization plan that balances planned and unplanned maintenance activities, updated weekly and visualized through a web application.
 5) Integrate the predictive solution with the internal OpsFlowsystem, incorporating a continuous feedback loop for ongoing improvement.

3. Additional details
 - A new Maintenance Plan is created each week, covering the next 360 days. This plan includes all planned and existing unplanned repairs and replacements.
 - The plans are entered into the OpsFlowSystem, which is then used by internal and external teams to accept, execute, and update the status of each job.
 - The Optimization Team is responsible for producing these weekly plans. They currently use Excel and rely on historical repair and replacement data, as well as manufacturer recommendations for component replacements, to build the 360-day forecast. According to the team, a planned job typically costs around €500, takes 2–3 hours, and is handled internally, while an unplanned job costs approximately €5,000, takes 1.5–2 days, and is usually performed by external contractors.


Deliverables
 1. Project plan following the **TDSP framework**.
 2. Critique the problem statement. Help the business to better frame the problem.
 3. Critique of the expected benefits. Help the business to understand potential additional benefits. Relate model metrics scenarios to cost savings.
 4. Proceed with your own assumptions whenever necessary.
 5. What additional information would you like to solicit from the business owners? What would you change in your project plan if in possession of that information?
 6. The previous elements should be contained in one slideshow, no more than 30 pages.
 
 7. A 15-minute online presentation followed by a 5-minute discussion.
 8. A single txt file is edited by each person (simply write your name) of each group on a feature branch and merged into a dev branch. A pull request, from dev to main is submitted with Andre Castro as the final reviewer. One repo per group.
